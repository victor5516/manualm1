2. TURN SIGNAL LIGHTS

  • Move turn signal switch up or down to the stop position to engage turn signals, and check for turn signal lights on.
  • When the turn is completed, the signal will cancel and the lever will return to the normal position.



3. HAZARD WARNING LIGHTS

  • Push the hazard warning flashes switch, and check that all turn signal lamps flash simultaneously.
  • Shorter flashes indicate the failure of lamp bulbs.
  • Push this switch again to turn them off.



4. BACK-UP LIGHTS

  • Select the gear shift lever to "R" position, and check for the lightening of back-up lights.

5. STOP LIGHTS

  • Foot the brake pedal, and check for the lightening of lower side of stop lights located in the rear combination lamp assembly.
  • Foot the brake pedal, and check for the lightening of center high mounted stop lamp (option) located in the tailgate.

6. TAIL LIGHTS, POSITION LIGHTS, LICENSE PLATE LIGHTS, ADDITIONAL TURN SIGNAL LIGHTS

  • Turn the light switch to position, and check for the lightening of tail, license plate, position lights, and additional turn signal lights.

  • Brake system warning lamp
    • Comes on when the parking brake is applied. If this light comes on even when the parking brake is completely released, it may indicate that the brake fluid in the reservoir is low.
  • Engine oil pressure warning lamp
    • Comes on when the ignition is switched on and goes out after the engine is started. If the light comes on while driving, it indicates that the oil pressure is dangerously low.
  • Charging system warning lamp
    • Comes on when the ignition is switched on and should go out when the engine is started.
  • Malfunction indicator lamp
    • Illuminates when the ignition is switched on and goes out shortly after the engine is running. If the indicator illuminates during normal running, a fault has occurred.
  • Coolant temperature waring lamp
    • Comes on when the engine coolant has over-heated.
  • Turn signal/hazard warning flasher
    • Flashes to indicate operation of the external turn signal or hazard warning flasher.
  • High beam indicator
    • Illuminates when the headlight high beam is switched on.
  • Door open warning lamp
    • Comes on when a door is either opened or not closed completely.
  • Safety belt reminder
    • Comes on when the driver does not fasten the safety belt.
  • Low fuel level warning lamp
    • Comes on when the quantity of fuel in fuel tank is below approx. 7.5L.
  • Selector lever position indicator (option)
    • Shows the shift position which the selector lever is in.
  • ABS warning lamp (option)
    • Illuminates when the ignition is switched on and goes out after about 2 seconds. If this light illuminates while driving, a malfunction of ABS is indicated.
  • Airbag warning lamp (option)
    • Flashes several times when the ignition is switched on and goes out. If this light illuminates or flashes while driving, a malfunction of airbag system is indicated.
  • Power mode indicator (option)
    • Illuminates whenever the ignition key is turned to "ON" and goes out. Also this indicator illuminates when power mode switch is pushed. If this indicator illuminates while driving without the switch being pressed, a fault in the automatic transmission is indicated.
  • Hold mode indicator (option)
    • Illuminates when the ignition key is turned to "ON" and goes out. Also this indicator illuminates when hold mode switch is pushed.
  • Front fog lamp indicator (option)
    • Comes on when the front fog lamps are turned on.
  • Rear fog lamp indicator (option)
    • Comes on when the rear fog lamps are turned on.
  • TCS operation indicator (option)
  • Illuminates when the ignition is ON and goes out after about 3 seconds along with the ABS warning lamp.
  • The TCS operation indicator blinks when traction control is working.



  • TCS warning lamp (option)
  • Illuminates when the ignition is ON and goes out after about 3 seconds along with the ABS warning lamp.
  • The TCS warning lamp illuminates to indicate that the system is not working because:
    • – there is a malfunctioning in the system or,
    • – you have canceled the system by pressing the TCS ON/ OFF button.



  • SSPS warning lamp (option)
  • Comes on when the ignition is ON and goes out after about 3 seconds.

    • HI = continuous wipe, fast speed

When you pull the lever for more than about 0.6 seconds, the washer fluid is sprayed onto the windshield while you pull the lever and then the windshield wipers are operated for several cycles.



  • Automatic wipe with rain sensor (option)
    • Check that the wipers operate smoothly during pouring water to near the top of windshield (rain sensor field) when the ignition switch is turned ON in AUTO position.

22. CLOCK

Check the operation of the clock by pressing the following button with ignition switch in the "ON" position.
  • H : Hour adjusting button
    • Press this button to adjust the hour digits
  • M : Minute adjusting button
    • Press this button to adjust the minute digits
  • S : To reset the time by the time signal, press this button.
    • For example, if this button is pressed while the time is between 8:00 and 8:29 the display is set to 8:00. If this button is pressed while the time is between 8:30 and 8:59, the display is set to 9:00.
    • 8:00 - 8:29 → 8:00
    • 8:30 - 8:59 → 9:00



Digital clock with outside temperature display (option)
  • H : Hour adjusting button
  • M : Minute adjusting button
  • T : Temperature display button
    • To display the outside temperature, press this button.
    • Display will change as follows:
    • Time → Celsius(°C) → Fahrenheit(°F)

28. SEAT

Check the operation of seats.
  • Front seat slide adjustment
    • To move the seat forwards or backwards, pull up the control lever located inboard and under the front of the seat and hold it. Then slide the seat to the desired position and release the lever.



  • Front seat reclining adjustment
    • To adjust the seat back, turn the hand wheel on the outboard side of the seat back until the desired angle is achieved.
    • For lever type relining adjustment, pull the lever up until the desired angle is achieved.



  • Driver's seat height adjustment (option)
    • To adjust driver's seat cushion angle, turn the hand wheels either way.



  • Head restraints
    • To adjust the front and rear seat head restraint, pull it up or push it down while pressing the release button.



    • To tilt the front head restraint, push it forward. The head restraint is returned to it's upright position when it is pushed all the way forward.



  • Seat lumbar support(option)
    • To adjust driver's or passenger's lumbar supports, turn the handwheel on the outboard side of seat back until the desired angle is achieved.



  • Power seat control switch(option)
    • – Seat slide adjustment
    • To move the seat forward or rearward, move the switch forward or rearward. Then, slide the seat to the desired position and release the switch.



    • – Seat height adjustment
    • To adjust the height of front part of seat cushion, push the front part of the switch up or down. To adjust the height of rear part of seat cushion, push the rear part of the switch up or down. To adjust the height of entire seat cushion, push the front and rear parts of switch simultaneously up or down.



    • – Seat reclining adjustment
    • To tilt the seatback forward or backward, move and hold the upper part of the switch forward or backward.



  • Turning front passenger's seat (option)
    • To turn the front passenger's seat rearwards, slide the seat fully backwards, adjust the seatback upright and turn it counter clockwise while pushing the turning lever outwards. (for RHD models, turn it clockwise)



  • Arm rests (option)
    • To use the armrests, pull the end of the armrests forward.






  • Foldable seatback table (option)
    • To use the seatback table, swing the end of the table up.



  • Folding rear seat back (option)
    • Pull the release lever on the top of each split seat back to unlock the seat back, then fold the seat back forward and down.



  • Double folding rear seatback (option)
    • Pull the strap at the rear side of each split seat cushion and lift the seat up.



    • Hold it using built-in hook.
    • The length of the strap is adjustable.



  • Removing rear seats (option)
    • Fold the rear seats forward, unlock the hinges on both sides of each split seat by pushing the release lever down and lift the seat out.



  • Center rear seat slide adjustment (option)
    • (3 piece type rear seat only)
    • Pull the lever at the front side of the seat cushion forward and hold it. Then slide the seat to the desired position and release the lever.



  • Rear seat relcliming adjustment (option)
    • (2 piece type rear seat only)
    • To tilt the rear seatback backward, remove the back shelf, pull the release handle and adjust the seatback to the desired position.

32. THEFT DETERRENT SYSTEM (OPTION)

  • Immobilizer system
    • Immobilizer system prevents the vehicle from being started by isolating the power supply to the ignition system, fuel pump and fuel injectors and it is a mechanical ignition key with integrated transponder, which is electronically coded. Check if the security indicator light comes on and goes out when the ignition is switched on.
  • Perimetric protection
    • – Setting the system
    • 1. Close all windows.
    • 2. Turn the ignition key to the "LOCK" position and remove it.
    • 3. Have all passengers get out of the vehicle.
    • 4. Close all doors, hood and tailgate. Lock all doors by pressing the lock button on the transmitter. At this time, the turn signal lights blink once, indicating all doors are locked.
    • 5. Confirm that the security indicator light in the right side of the digital clock start flashing.
    • 6. The security light begins to flash once every 0.8 seconds(0.1 second : ON, 0.7 second : OFF). The system is now activated.
    • – Testing the system
    • 1. Open all the windows.
    • 2. Set the system as described in "setting the system". The front doors should be locked with the transmitter. Be sure to wait until the security indicator light starts.
    • 3. Unlock one of the doors using the inside door lock. Then the system should activate the alarm.
    • 4. Cancel the system by unlocking either front door with the key or the transmitter.
    • 5. Repeat this operation for the other doors, trunk lid and hood. When testing on the hood, also check that the system is activated when the battery terminal is disconnected and then reconnected.

33. KEYLESS ENTRY SYSTEM WITH ANTITHEFT FUNCTION(OPTION)

Check the operation of the keyless entry system by using the transmitter.
Door lock and antitheft mode activation
Press the door lock button on the transmitter and release it. LED on the transmitter flashes once.
Then all the doors are locked, hazard warning` lamps flashes once with chirp sound and the antitheft mode is activated.
If the ignition switch is placed in the "ON" position, the antitheft mode and hazard warning lamps do not operate.
To release the antitheft mode, press the door unlock button, and release it on the transmitter or open doors by key.
Door unlock and antitheft mode deactivation
Press the door unlock button on the transmitter and release it. LED on the transmitter flashes once.
Then all the doors are unlocked, hazard warning lamps flash twice and the antitheft mode is deactivated.
But, when the ignition switch is placed in the "ON" position, the hazard warning lamps do not flash.
Auto door lock
If the door is not opened or the engine is not started within 30 seconds after unlocking the door with the transmitter when the vehicle is under the antitheft mode, all the doors are automatically locked and the antitheft mode is switched on.
Antitheft alarm function
When the vehicle is under antitheft mode, for 30 seconds the alarm sounds and the hazard warning lamps flashes in the following cases.
  1. When the door is opened, not by using the transmitter or a ignition key, but by any other methods.
  2. When starting the engine with a ignition key or any other methods without releasing the antitheft mode.
To stop the alarm and hazard warning flasher, press any button on the transmitter.
