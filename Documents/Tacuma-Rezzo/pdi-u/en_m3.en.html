2. TURN SIGNAL LIGHTS

  • Move turn signal switch up or down to the stop position to engage turn signals, and check for turn signal lights on.
  • When the turn is completed, the signal will cancel and the lever will return to the normal position.



3. HAZARD WARNING LIGHTS

  • Push the hazard warning flashes switch, and check that all turn signal lamps flash simultaneously.
  • Shorter flashes indicate the failure of lamp bulbs.
  • Push this switch again to turn them off.



4. BACK-UP LIGHTS

  • Select the gear shift lever to "R" position, and check for the lightening of back-up lights.

5. STOP LIGHTS

  • Foot the brake pedal, and check for the lightening of upper side of stop lights located in the rear combination lamp assembly.
  • Foot the brake pedal, and check for the lightening of center high mounted stop lamp (option) located on the tailgate.

  • Brake system warning light
    • Comes on when the parking brake is applied. If this light comes on even when the parking brake is completely released, it may indicate that the brake fluid in the reservoir is low.
  • Engine oil pressure warning light
    • Comes on when the ignition is switched on and goes out after the engine is started. If the light comes on while driving, it indicates that the oil pressure is dangerously low.
  • Alternator warning light
    • Comes on when the ignition is switched on and should go out when the engine is started.
  • Engine control indicator
    • Illuminates when the ignition is switched on and should go out after about 4 seconds. If the indicator illuminates during normal running, a fault has occurred.
  • Turn signal/hazard warning flasher
    • Flashes to indicate operation of the external turn signal or hazard warning flasher.
  • High beam indicator
    • Illuminates when the headlight high beam is switched on.
  • Seat belt reminder
    • Comes on when the driver does not fasten the seat belt.
  • Low fuel level warning light
    • Comes on when the quantity of fuel in fuel tank is below approx. 5.5L.
  • ABS warning light (option)
    • Illuminates when the ignition is switched on and should go out after about 4 seconds. If this light illuminates while driving, a malfunction of ABS is detected.



  • Airbag warning light (option)
    • Illuminates for approx. 4 seconds when the ignition is switched on and should go out. If this light illuminates while driving, a malfunction of airbag system is detected.



  • Rear fog lamp indicator
    • Comes on when the rear fog lamp is turned on.



8. DOME LIGHTS

Check for the function of centre dome lamp.
  • The lamp should operate as follows.
    • – "ON" position : Always comes on and stays on.
    • – "OFF" position : The light remains off even when a door is opened.
    • – Set the switch to "DOOR" position (between "ON" and "OFF" ), and check that the light comes on when a door is opened.



9. HEADLAMP LEVELING SWITCH (OPTION)

With the low beam switched on, adjust head lamp range to suit the vehicle load.
  • 0 = Front seat occupied
  • 1 = All seats occupied
  • 2 = All seats occupied and load in the boot



10. INSTRUMENT PANEL GAUGE OPERATION

Check for the operation of the following instrument panel gauges.
  • Fuel gauge
    • Indicates the level of fuel remaining in the fuel tank. The gauge continues to indicate the fuel level even after the ignition is turned off. When the fuel is added, the gauge slowly changes to the new fuel level after the ignition is turned on.
  • Temperature gauge
    • Indicates the temperature of engine coolant when the ignition switch is in the "II" position.

12. REAR WINDOW DEMISTER SWITCH (WITHOUT TIMER : OPTION)

14. HORN SWITCH

Press the center of the steering wheel (without driver's airbag), or far right or left of the steering wheel (with driver's airbag) and check the function of the horn.



15. WIPER AND WASHER OPERATION

  • Windshield wiper switch
    • Check that the wipers operate smoothly after moving the lever upwards with the ignition on.
    • OFF = off
    • INT = intermittent wiper operation(once every 4 seconds)
    • LOW = continuous wipe, slow speed
    • HIGH = continuous wipe, fast speed
    • – Misting function
    • To operate the wipers once, lightly move the lever towards the INT position and release it.



  • Windshield washer switch
    • Pull the lever towards the steering wheel for less than about 0.5 seconds and check if the washer fluid is sprayed onto the windshield with the windshield wipers not being operated. When you pull the lever for more than about 0.5 seconds, the washer fluid is sprayed onto the windshield while you pull the lever and then the windshield wipers are operated for several cycles.



16. TAILGATE WINDOW WIPER AND WASHER SWITCH (OPTION)

  • Push lever away from steering wheel and check the function;
  • First position (rest position) = wiper action
  • Second position (rocker position) = wiper and washer

18. CIGARETTE LIGHTER

Check the operation of the cigarette lighter. Push the lighter in with the ignition switch in the "I" or "II" position. It will automatically pop out when it becomes heated.

Note : If the lighter does not pop out within 30 seconds, pull it out and check it.



19. CLOCK

Check the operation of the clock by pressing the following button with ignition switch in the "II" position.
  • H : Hour adjusting button
    • Press this button to adjust the hour digits
  • M : Minute adjusting button
    • Press this button to adjust the minute digits
  • S : To reset the time by the time signal, press this button.
    • For example, if this button is pressed while the time is between 8:00 and 8:29 the display is set to 8:00. If this button is pressed while the time is between 8:30 and 8:59, the display is set to 9:00.
    • 8:00 – 8:29 → 8:00
    • 8:30 – 8:59 → 9:00



20. DOOR LOCK

Check doors being locked or unlocked from outside by using the key.
All doors can be locked or unlocked from the inside by pushing or pulling the door lock buttons located on the window sill on each door. The driver's door cannot be locked from the outside by pushing the door lock button, thus preventing the driver from being inadvertently locked out.






  • Central door locking system (option)
    • All doors can be simultaneously locked or unlocked when the driver's door is locked or unlocked.
  • Child security door locks
    • Check if the rear door cannot be opened from the inside when the metal latch beneath the lock on either of the rear doors is pushed downwards.



21. DOOR WINDOWS

  • Manual door window
    • Check the operation of the door windows by rotating the window regulator handle in the door panel.



  • Power window switches (option)
    • Operate the power window switches on the center console when the ignition switch is in "II" position.
    • To lower the window, keep the top part of the switch pressed.
    • To raise the window, keep the top part of the switch lifted up. When the window reaches the desired position, release it.



22. OUTSIDE REARVIEW MIRRORS

Check the operation of outside rearview mirrors by using adjusting levers (option) or simply moving the mirror.






23. MANUAL SUN ROOF (OPTION)

Check the operation of manual sum roof.
To unlock, pull the sun roof handle towards the front of the vehicle.
To open, press the button and raise the sunroof to the desired position.
To close, press the button and pull the sun roof handle down.



24. SEAT

Check the operation of seats.
  • Front seat slide adjustment
    • To move the seat forwards or backwards, pull up the control lever located under the front side of the front seat and hold it. Then slide the seat to the desired position and release the lever.



  • Front seat reclining adjustment
    • To adjust the seat back, pull up the lever on the outboard side of the seat cushion until the desired angle is achieved and release the lever.



  • Head restraints
    • To adjust the front seat head restraint, pull it up or push it down.



  • Folding rear seat back
    • Lift up the center of seat cushion and position it uprightly. Pull up the locking knob on the top of seat back to unlock the seat back, then fold the seat back forward and down.






25. FUEL FILLER DOOR RELEASE LEVER

Stop the engine and open the fuel filler door by pulling up the fuel filler door release lever located under the left-side of the driver's seat.



26. TAILGATE RELEASE LEVER (OPTION)

Check the operation of the tailgate release lever located under the left-side of the driver's seat.



27. BONNET RELEASE LEVER

To open the bonnet, pull the release handle located on left lower side of instrument panel to unlatch the bonnet. And push the bottom of the hook upwards and lift the bonnet.






To hold the bonnet in the open position, insert the support rod into the slot provided in the upperside of the engine room.



28. IMMOBILIZER SYSTEM (OPTION)

Immobilizer system prevents the vehicle from being started by unauthorized persons by isolating the power supply to the ignition system, fuel pump and fuel injectors and it is a mechanical ignition key with integrated transponder, which is electronically coded. Check if the security indicator light comes on and goes out when the ignition is switched on.
